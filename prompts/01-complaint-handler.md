# Prompt 1 — Complaint Handler (FREE)
## HEARD Framework Complaint Response Generator

---

## The Prompt

```
You are a customer service expert trained in the HEARD framework (Hear, Empathize, Apologize, Resolve, Diagnose) — the same method used by Disney, Zappos, and Ritz-Carlton to turn angry customers into loyal ones.

Write a professional, empathetic complaint response email for the following situation:

**Business:** [Business name and type — e.g., "PetBox, a monthly pet supply subscription box"]
**Customer name:** [First name]
**What went wrong:** [Describe the complaint — e.g., "Order arrived 8 days late with a damaged item. Customer is furious and threatening to cancel."]
**Resolution options available:** [What you can offer — e.g., "Full refund, replacement shipment, or store credit + 20% off next box"]
**Tone:** [Professional and warm / Casual and friendly / Formal / Other]
**Company policy notes:** [Any relevant policy — e.g., "We offer free replacements within 30 days of delivery"]

---

Apply the HEARD framework in this exact structure:

**H — Hear (1-2 sentences):** Acknowledge that you received and read their message. Reference the specific issue (not generic). Do NOT start with "I'm sorry."

**E — Empathize (2-3 sentences):** Mirror the customer's emotional experience. Use feeling language. Show you understand the impact, not just the inconvenience.

**A — Apologize (1-2 sentences):** Offer a calibrated apology. Take ownership without admitting legal negligence. Avoid: "sorry for any inconvenience," "we apologize for the trouble," or generic corporate-speak.

**R — Resolve (2-4 sentences):** Present the resolution clearly. Give them a choice if possible (2-3 options). State what you're doing right now (active, not passive). Set a clear timeline.

**D — Diagnose (1-2 sentences):** Briefly explain what happened or what you're doing to prevent recurrence. Shows the customer this won't happen again.

**Recovery gesture:** Add a goodwill offer (discount, free item, extended trial) calibrated to the severity of the complaint. High severity = more generous offer.

**Closing:** Warm close that invites them to reply if anything isn't right. Include direct contact name and method.

---

Output:
1. Subject line (3 variants — different angles: empathy / action / ownership)
2. Full email body using HEARD framework
3. Tone notes: 1 sentence on when to adjust formality
4. Follow-up note: what to send if they don't reply in 48 hours (2 sentences)
```

---

## Tips for Best Results

- **Be specific about what went wrong** — the more detail, the more personalized the output
- **Include your actual resolution options** — the prompt will present them naturally in the email
- **Name the customer** — Claude uses their name in the right places for warmth
- **Calibrate the apology to severity:** minor delay → light apology; missing birthday gift → fuller ownership

---

## When to Use This

- Customer complains via email about a product, delivery, or service failure
- Negative review left on Google, Trustpilot, or Yelp (adapt for public response)
- Post-purchase survey comes back with a complaint narrative
- Customer is threatening to dispute the charge or cancel

---

## What NOT to Include

- Don't admit specific liability ("it was our fault the driver crashed")
- Don't promise outcomes you can't guarantee ("this will never happen again")
- Don't use passive voice for the apology ("mistakes were made")
- Don't offer discounts so large they set a precedent (cap goodwill at 30% or a free replacement)
