# Example Output: PetBox Subscription Box
## All 4 Prompts — End-to-End Worked Example

**Business:** PetBox — monthly subscription box for dogs and cats ($39.99/month)  
**Scenario arc:** Missing delivery → defective item complaint → escalation → proactive win-back

---

## Prompt 1 — Complaint Handler

**Input:**
- Business: PetBox (monthly pet supply subscription)
- Customer: Sarah
- What went wrong: Box arrived 9 days late (normal is 5 days), treats were moldy, chew toy was cracked/sharp edge. Customer has a 2-year-old Golden Retriever named Cooper. She's very upset and posted on Instagram.
- Resolution options: Full refund, replacement box shipped priority, or store credit + 30% off next 3 months
- Tone: Warm and genuine — not corporate

---

**Output:**

**Subject line variants:**
1. "Sarah, I read your message about Cooper's box — here's what I'm doing right now"
2. "Your July PetBox: I owe you an explanation and a solution"  
3. "Cooper deserves better than this — so do you"

---

**Email:**

Hi Sarah,

Thank you for taking the time to write to us — I read every word of your message, and I want to make this right.

A 9-day wait, a moldy bag of treats, and a cracked toy with a sharp edge on it — I don't want to minimize any of that. Cooper trusts you to keep him safe, and a toy with a broken edge is exactly the kind of thing no pet parent should have to find. I'm genuinely sorry your July box showed up this way.

This is on us. The delay originated from our fulfillment partner's distribution center in Phoenix — we had a routing issue during the heatwave that we should have caught sooner, and the treats were part of a batch that didn't pass our temperature protocol during transit. We've pulled that supplier's current stock and flagged the toy lot for inspection. Neither of those products should have made it to you.

Here's what I'd like to do: I'm sending you a replacement box today — priority shipping, so you'll have it by Thursday. I've flagged Cooper's profile to ensure the treats in this replacement are from our verified cold-chain vendors only. I'm also adding 30% off your next three months of PetBox automatically — no code needed, already applied to your account.

If you'd prefer a full refund of your July box instead of the replacement, just reply and I'll process it within the hour. You shouldn't have to wait any longer than you already have.

The July box was Cooper's welcome to PetBox, and it was a bad one. I want to earn the chance to show you what we're actually like.

Warmly,  
Megan  
PetBox Customer Care  
Direct: hello@petbox.co | (Note: reply to this email — I'll personally follow up)

---

**Tone note:** This email uses "I" language throughout (not "we") to feel personal — adjust to "we" for mass responses, but keep "I" for direct one-on-one complaints.

**Follow-up note:** If Sarah doesn't respond in 48 hours, send: "Hi Sarah — just checking that my email from [date] reached you. Did you want the replacement box or the refund? Either way, the 30% discount is already on your account."

## Prompt 2 — Return / Refund / Exchange (Deny + Alternative)

**Input:**
- Business: PetBox
- Customer: Marcus
- Order: January PetBox, purchased 4.5 months ago, $39.99
- Return reason: "I never opened the box. I lost my dog in February. Can I return it?"
- Decision: DENY (outside 30-day window) — but offer compassionate alternative
- Policy: 30-day returns, unopened only
- Goodwill offer: Full store credit + option to donate a box to a shelter in their dog's name

---

**Subject line variants:**
1. "Marcus — a note about your January box"
2. "About your return request — and an idea I'd like to offer"
3. "Your January PetBox — I'm so sorry"

---

**Email:**

Hi Marcus,

I received your message, and before I say anything else — I'm so sorry for the loss of your dog. That kind of grief is real and it takes time, and I want to be thoughtful with you.

Regarding the January box: our return policy covers unopened orders within 30 days of shipment, and at this point we're outside that window. I can't process a traditional refund, and I want to be honest with you about that rather than give you the runaround.

What I'd like to offer instead:

**Option 1:** Full store credit — $39.99 in your PetBox account, valid for 12 months. No expiration pressure. If you bring another dog into your life someday, it's there. If you don't, it's there for a gift to someone whose pet would love it.

**Option 2:** Donate a box in your dog's name. We partner with 3 Las Vegas-area rescues and shelters. I'll ship your January box directly to one of them — your full $39.99 goes to dogs who need it, and we'll send you a donation confirmation with your dog's name on it.

I'll also pause your subscription immediately — no charge will run until you tell me you're ready, if ever.

You've been with PetBox for over a year, Marcus. That matters to us. Whatever you decide, your account credit is there whenever you need it.

With care,  
Megan  
PetBox Customer Care

---

**Escalation flag:** If Marcus responds with anger or a chargeback threat, escalate to supervisor immediately — this is a compassionate situation and any hard-line policy enforcement will go viral.

## Prompt 3 — Escalation (Social Media Crisis)

**Input:**
- Business: PetBox
- Situation: Customer @sarahlovescoop posted an Instagram story: "PetBox sent moldy treats and a broken toy to my dog. Going to report them to the BBB." Story has been up for 3 hours, 12 DMs asking "is PetBox safe?"
- Resolution authorized: Full replacement + 30% off next 3 months
- What we cannot offer: Refund + replacement (choose one only)

---

**Public Instagram comment response (under their story):**

Hi Sarah — we saw your story and I'm so sorry about Cooper's box. This is not okay and we're fixing it right now. I've just sent you a DM so we can take care of this directly. — Megan, PetBox Care Team

*(Under 3 sentences. Doesn't get defensive. Shows other viewers you respond fast and take it seriously.)*

---

**Private DM follow-up:**

Hi Sarah, I'm Megan from the PetBox care team — I caught your story and I wanted to reach you directly.

The moldy treats and the cracked toy shouldn't have left our facility. I pulled up your account and I can see the delay was our fault on the routing side, and the treats and toy were from a batch we've now flagged and pulled.

I want to make this right: I'm sending a replacement box via priority shipping today — everything fresh, and I've added a note to your account about Cooper's size and breed so the toy selection is appropriate. I've also added 30% off your next three months automatically — no code, already in your account.

If there's anything about the replacement that isn't perfect for Cooper, I'm at this DM. And thank you for posting — we needed to know.

Megan | PetBox Care Team

---

**Request to update the post (add to the DM once resolved):**

"Sarah — I'm really glad we could sort this out for Cooper. If you feel comfortable, would you be open to adding a note to your original story that we reached out and made it right? Totally no pressure — just want other dog parents to know we take this seriously. Either way, happy Cooper is what matters."

---

**Risk level:** High — Instagram with 12 visible "is PetBox safe?" comments = potential viral spread. Resolution within 2 hours is critical.

**What NOT to say publicly:** Don't explain the fulfillment center issue in the public comment. Don't offer compensation publicly. Don't say "we take quality very seriously" (cliché that makes things worse).

## Prompt 4 — Proactive Service: Order Delay Notification

**Input:**
- Business: PetBox
- Customer: Generic batch template (all July subscribers affected)
- Outreach type: Order delay — fulfillment center routing issue, new ETA is 9 days instead of 5
- Gesture: $5 store credit + free month if box doesn't arrive by new ETA
- Tone: Warm and direct

---

**Subject line variants:**
1. "Your July PetBox is running late — here's what we know"
2. "A delay we want you to hear from us first"
3. "Your July box: new arrival date + something from us"

---

**Email:**

Hi [First Name],

We noticed your July PetBox hasn't shipped on schedule, and we'd rather tell you now than have you check tracking and wonder.

A routing issue at our Phoenix fulfillment center delayed your box — your new estimated delivery is [New ETA Date], which is about 4 extra days. We're working with the carrier to expedite where possible.

We know your pet was expecting this — so we're adding $5 in store credit to your account automatically as a thank-you for your patience. And if the box doesn't arrive by [New ETA + 1 day], reply to this email and we'll give you next month free, no questions asked.

If the new date doesn't work for you and you'd like to cancel this month's box, just reply and we'll process a full refund for July.

We'll send a shipping confirmation the moment it leaves the warehouse. Thanks for being patient with us.

— The PetBox Team

---

**Timing guidance:** Send within 6 hours of detecting the delay — before the customer checks tracking. "We knew before you" is the core message.

**Segmentation note:** For first-time subscribers, personalize the subject line and add an extra apology line: "We know this is your first box with us, and we're not off to the start we wanted." High-value subscribers (6+ months) should get a direct note from a named person, not a team signature.

**A/B test suggestion:** Test Subject Line 1 (transparent/factual) vs. Subject Line 2 (empathy-forward) — expect transparent to win in open rate, empathy to win in click-through.
