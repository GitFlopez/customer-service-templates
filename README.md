## The Problem

Bad customer service costs US businesses $75 billion a year in lost revenue.

Most small businesses lose customers not because of bad products — but because:
- They respond too slowly or too robotically
- They say "sorry for the inconvenience" (customers hate this)
- They deny returns in ways that feel like a fight
- They wait for customers to complain instead of getting ahead of problems
- They don't know what to say when someone threatens a chargeback or goes nuclear on social

This skill fixes all of it.

## What You Get

### Prompt 1 — Complaint Handler (Free)
HEARD framework complaint response. Acknowledges, empathizes, resolves, recovers. Full email + 3 subject line variants.

### Prompt 2 — Return / Refund / Exchange Script
Policy-first but empathy-forward. Approval path, denial path, exchange upsell, re-engagement offer. Turns returns into retention moments.

### Prompt 3 — Escalation & Difficult Customer Playbook
De-escalation email, supervisor request, social media crisis redirect, legal threat response, repeat complainant boundary-setting. Keeps you out of legal trouble and off Trustpilot.

### Prompt 4 — Proactive Service Scripts
Order delay, shipping issue, defect notification, post-bad-experience win-back, loyalty milestone, NPS recovery. Get ahead of problems before customers know they exist.
